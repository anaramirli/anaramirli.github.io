My insights on how the future technology will be shaped by the needs of AI and the changes it brings.

The desire of inventors to create machines that think dates back to ancient times. Even more than 2.500 years before, Greek inventors were imagining robotic servants, animated statues and other forms of artificial intelligence (AI) [[1]](#1). When early programmable computers were first designed, inventors wondered whether such machines might become as competent as humans. However, this matter still remains as a subtle enigma. The most recent advancements in computer software and hardware and the ever-increasing amount of data have made artificial intelligence the primary domain of today's technology with many effective applications and active research topics. Today, AI can be defined as the basic simulation of natural human intelligence by a computer system. The processes here involve learning, reasoning, and self-correction [[2-4]](#3). The main applications include automating routine labour, understanding sounds and images, analyzing, and making a diagnosis. Although current researches are conducted with a focus on solving intuitive problems, it lays the groundwork for the future role of AI. Researchers believe there is a high probability of AI outperforming humans in all tasks in almost half-century from now [[5]](#5). Achieving this, AI will transform modern society by reshaping healthcare, transportation, science exploration, sport, finance, art, and the military.

In the early stages of artificial intelligence, researchers tried to solve problems that are computationally straightforward to resolve. Many of these problems took place in a formal and simple environment which can be explained by a set of mathematical rules and logical terms. So that many early successful artificial agents did not require to have extensive knowledge about the world. IBM’s Deep Blue chess machine beating Garry Kasparov in 1997 can be the best example of how some environments can be described by a very brief set of formal rules [[6]](#6). Throughout the research on artificial intelligence, several approaches designed to reason some statements about the world in formal language - methods known as the knowledge base models [[7]](#7). Later experiments showed that knowledge-based models failed to acquire logical inference about the environment. However, with the extensive research on machine learning concepts, AI gained the ability to harness knowledge by extracting patterns from raw data. Consequently, newly developed paradigms of machine learning, especially modern Artificial Neural Networks, led to seminal advances in the field. Today, machine learning powers many aspects of our society: from the human assistance systems to face recognition system on our smartphones. But it was with the introduction of Deep Learning that researchers took a big step toward the dream of General AI that they have long dreamed of. This method has dramatically improved the state-of-the-art in many applications [[8-11]](#10). Now computers are able to learn complicated concepts by gathering knowledge from experience. However, there are still many open challenges existing in front of artificial intelligence to reach and even surpass human-level performance. 

Unarguably, deep neural networks transformed AI research like never before. Therefore, observing the present research trends in deep learning, we can have great insights into the future of artificial intelligence. Today's research in deep learning mainly focuses to find an answer to two main challenges. The first challenge is how a learning agent can build a good representation of the world to control every aspect of it while observing the world. Currently, a number of experiments are being conducted on this issue to imitate general human intelligence [[12,13]](#12). The second one is how a learning agent can learn just like a human does by comparison without the need to an extensive amount of data to process [[14-16]](#15). If researchers realize these general tasks, undoubtedly AI will exceed its current limited abilities in greater extent. Nevertheless, present-day trends imply that AI will have radical effects on future technology no matter what.

With the help of new paradigms in algorithms, the introduction of cloud computing and an immense amount of data, AI has already started to play a primary role in technological development. Today, we see AI in everywhere - from Amazon’s virtual assistant Alexa to the self-driving capability of Tesla. It has already proved its capacity to be adapted to numerous domains: offering accurate tumour detection [[17,18]](#17), providing intraoperative navigation and robotic-assisted surgery [[19]](#19), reducing pollution in agriculture with smart pesticide spraying system in dynamic environments [[20]](#20), analyzing climate change to overcome global warming and energy consumption problems. As AI's capacity to provide assistance in human tasks improves, it starts to be a more dominant figure in daily life. AI begins to integrate into every aspect of our lives from business to our daily life. As a matter of fact, a review of many experts in the field indicates that human labour will be almost replaced by the autonomous systems within a century [[5]](#5). AI is considered as one of the most important things humanity is working on. Developments in AI not only lead to changes in ways we interact with technology but also set to change major technologies such as software and hardware systems. One of the effective ways to understand AI’s future influence is to investigate the computer systems.

Thanks to recent developments in software systems that most of today's AI paradigms are now applicable. The modern digital world requires engineers to move with the demands of extensive and sustainable software approaches for complex real-world problems such as information technology security. One of these emerging frontiers is cyber-security. Many researchers are trying to use AI for the protection of data in the digital world, extending the ways to response cyber attacks, and even using it for counter cyber attacks [[21-23]](#22). Another domain is programming in which researchers are exploring to tackle complex problems without explicit programming. However, the built-in code generation capability in AI systems requires a new architecture to perform fully autonomous code generation without any explicit programming. This concern rises a need for more complex development tools. Andrej Karpathy, the director of artificial intelligence and Autopilot Vision at Tesla, suggests that programming will drive the adaptation of complex machine-generated codes with the introduction of more abstract programming languages formed by deep learning models [[24]](#24). Indeed, a number of researchers have already successfully used generative AI models to create and explore new AI architectures [[25-27]](#26). Current research trends show that the combination of code generation technologies and machine learning will improve the programming in such a way that most of the machines will write their own codes without explicit programming [[28]](#28). No wonder, the ever-increasing complexity of these domains will bring more complex software architectures.

The new generations of networks technologies and mobile applications have aslo started to transform our approach toward technology. All these developments accelerate progress in robotics as well as in Internet of things (IoT) which enable us to communicate and interact with physical devices over the Internet. On the other hand, the cost of computation and storage for deep neural networks and other complex AI paradigms are usually high to deploy them into to the devices with limited computing capabilities such as wearables devices, smartphones, and other embedded systems. As the complexity of deep neural networks increases, it becomes more expensive to compute these algorithms with limited devices. In recent years, two main directions of study have been explored to reduce the cost of computation: the development of optimal designs for hardware architecture systems that are able to operate complex computations [[29]](#29) and the reduction in the cost of the neural network by changing neural networks structure [[30, 31]](#30). As a matter of fact, it seems, AI not only will bring effective gains in robotics and IoT, but it will also be a nontrivial reason for the advances in hardware systems.

The need for more profound and competent AI systems triggers fundamental changes in hardware and software architecture. Which in its turns boosts up the technological developments. Within many years, AI expected to perform some human-level tasks. Our devices will be in a position to do action on behalf of us. We’ll see more competent self-controlled vehicles on roads, more AI-powered assistance systems in our lives, and more precise predictions made by computer systems.
